# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: February 1, 2026**

Academic Satire ("we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website academicsatire.com (the "Site").

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide when you:

- **Subscribe to our newsletter**: Email address and name
- **Contact us**: Name, email address, and message content
- **Comment on blog posts**: Name, email address, and comment content

### 1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our Site, we automatically collect certain information:

- **Device Information**: Browser type, operating system, device type
- **Usage Data**: Pages visited, time spent on pages, click patterns
- **Location Data**: General geographic location based on IP address
- **Cookies and Similar Technologies**: See Section 4 below

### 1.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information from:

- **Analytics Providers**: Google Analytics provides aggregated website usage data
- **Social Media Platforms**: If you interact with our social media content

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to:

- Deliver newsletter content you've subscribed to
- Respond to your inquiries and communications
- Improve our website and content
- Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
- Prevent fraud and ensure website security
- Comply with legal obligations

### Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), we process your data based on:

- **Consent**: Newsletter subscriptions and optional cookies
- **Legitimate Interest**: Website analytics and improvement
- **Contractual Necessity**: Fulfilling your requests
- **Legal Obligation**: Compliance with applicable laws

### 7.1 For All Users

You have the right to:

- **Right to Withdraw Consent**: At any time, without affecting prior processing

California residents have the right to:

- Know what personal information is collected
- Know if personal information is sold or disclosed
